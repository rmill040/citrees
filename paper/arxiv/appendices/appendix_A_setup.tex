\subsection{Setup and notation}
\label{app:setup}

Let $(X_i, Y_i)_{i=1}^n$ be training data and consider a fixed node $t$ with
index set $I_t$ (size $n_t$). Write $X_t := (X_i)_{i \in I_t}$ and
$Y_t := (Y_i)_{i \in I_t}$.

At node $t$, \citrees performs two (potential) hypothesis-testing steps:
Stage~A screens features $j \in F_t$ for association with $Y_t$, and Stage~B
tests candidate thresholds for the selected feature $j_t^\star$.

\subsection{Assumptions and scope ledger}
\label{app:assumptions}

We label assumptions to keep statements and proofs aligned. When a result below
is described as ``fixed-node,'' it means the node $t$ is treated as fixed (not
adaptively selected by the data).

\paragraph{A0.1 (Fixed node).}\label{assump:fixed-node}
The node $t$ is fixed, not data-adaptively selected.

\paragraph{A0.2 (Conditional exchangeability).}\label{assump:exchangeability}
Under the null being tested, $Y_t$ is exchangeable conditional on $(X_t, U)$,
where $U$ denotes label-independent algorithmic randomness (e.g.,
\texttt{max\_features}, threshold subsampling, RNG seeds).

\paragraph{A0.3 (Label-independent tested family).}\label{assump:label-independence}
The tested feature family $F_t$, candidate thresholds, and the resample budget
$B$ are measurable functions of $(X_t, U)$ and do \emph{not} depend on the
observed labels $Y_t$ (or on permutation outcomes).

\paragraph{A0.4 (Fixed-$B$ p-values).}\label{assump:fixed-b}
Permutation p-values are computed with a fixed number of resamples $B$ (no
optional stopping).

\paragraph{A0.5 (Tie handling).}\label{assump:ties}
Ties are either (i) counted against the null (conservative) or (ii) resolved by
randomized tie-breaking for exact uniformity.

\paragraph{A0.6 (Idealized symmetry model for CART lemmas).}\label{assump:cart-symmetry}
In idealized CART-bias lemmas (not used for paper-facing guarantees), candidate
split scores are assumed exchangeable across all $(j,c)$ pairs (and tie-free, or
tie-broken at random).

\paragraph{A0.7 (Impurity moment bounds).}\label{assump:impurity-moments}
When bounding expected impurity decreases under nulls, assume bounded root
impurity (classification) or finite second moments as stated.

\paragraph{A0.8 (Honest estimation).}\label{assump:honesty}
For honest estimation statements, the sample split $(S, E)$ is independent of
the observed data.
