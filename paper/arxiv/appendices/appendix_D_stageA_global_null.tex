\section{Proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:stageA-global-null}}
\label{app:proof-stageA-global}

\paragraph{Claim.} Proposition~\ref{prop:stageA-global-null}.
\paragraph{Assumptions used.} A0.1--A0.5 (\cref{app:assumptions}).

Stage~A rejects the complete (global) null only if
$\min_{j \in F_t} p_{t,j} < \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}/m_t$. Under the complete (global) null,
each $p_{t,j}$ is super-uniform. By A0.3, the tested family $F_t$ and its
cardinality $m_t$ are measurable with respect to $(X_t,U)$, so we can condition
on $(X_t,U)$ and treat $(F_t,m_t)$ as fixed. The union bound gives
%
\begin{equation}
  \begin{aligned}
    \PP\!\left(\min_{j \in F_t} p_{t,j} < \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}/m_t \mid X_t,U\right)
    &\le \sum_{j \in F_t} \PP(p_{t,j} < \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}/m_t \mid X_t,U) \\
    &\le \sum_{j \in F_t} \PP(p_{t,j} \le \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}/m_t \mid X_t,U) \\
    &\le m_t \cdot \frac{\alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}}{m_t}
    = \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}.
  \end{aligned}
\end{equation}
%
Taking expectations over $(X_t,U)$ yields the stated unconditional bound.
