\subsection{Bootstrap and rank checks}

\Cref{tab:cif-ci-comparisons} gives the CIF comparisons
against ctree, cforest, and CIT after restricting to datasets with all methods,
downstream learners, and standard $k$ values available. Within each task, all
comparisons use the same datasets, the same downstream models, and
$k \in \{5,10,25,50,100\}$.

  downstream learners and values of $k$. The metric is balanced accuracy for
  classification and $R^2$ for regression. The confidence intervals are
  percentile bootstrap intervals from 20{,}000 replicates. Positive differences
  favor CIF. The sign test alternative is CIF $>$ comparator. W--L gives wins
  and losses; no exact ties occurred in these comparisons. Large positive
  regression differences can reflect negative mean $R^2$ for the comparator.}
  \label{tab:cif-ci-comparisons}
\end{table}

The same classification benchmark with 14 datasets yields a Friedman test statistic
of
$\chi^2(16)=141.32$ with $p<0.001$ and Kendall's
$W=0.63$. The analogous regression calculation uses the 6 datasets where all
methods, downstream learners, and standard $k$ values are present. The
corresponding rank summaries are a Friedman statistic of $\chi^2(17)=33.79$, an
asymptotic $p$-value of 0.0089, and Kendall's $W=0.33$. Because this regression
analysis includes 6 datasets, the asymptotic $p$-value should be read
cautiously.

\subsection{Sensitivity to downstream learner, k, and seed}

The main benchmark averages over downstream learners, standard values of $k$,
folds, and seeds. The following tables report method ordering separately by
downstream learner, value of $k$, and seed as descriptive sensitivity analyses.
They use the same selected method configurations as the main benchmark.

For the learner and $k$ checks, each comparison unit is one downstream learner
and one standard value of $k$. Within a unit, methods are ordered by their mean
rank within datasets with every compared method available for that
unit; ties use average ranks.
\Cref{tab:classification-learner-k-sensitivity,tab:regression-learner-k-sensitivity}
summarize the 15 learner and $k$ combinations for each task. The
learner columns report mean position over the five standard values of $k$ for
that learner; lower positions are better. Mean position averages the positions
obtained after ordering methods within each learner and $k$ combination; mean
rank averages the dataset-level ranks directly. The Top 3 and Top 5 counts
report learner and $k$ combinations, not datasets.
