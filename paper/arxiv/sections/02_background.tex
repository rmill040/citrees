\section{Background and related work}
\label{sec:background}

Conditional inference trees were introduced by \citet{Hothorn2006UnbiasedRecursivePartitioning}
as an alternative to greedy impurity optimization, replacing variable selection
with hypothesis testing to reduce selection bias. A separate literature studies
bias in tree-based importance measures (e.g., in random forests) and proposes
corrections \citep{Strobl2007BiasRFVariableImportance}.

Permutation tests provide a nonparametric route to calibration under an
exchangeability assumption. In Monte Carlo permutation testing, the Phipson--Smyth
``+1'' correction ensures p-values are never exactly zero and yields valid
finite-sample super-uniformity under exchangeability \citep{PhipsonSmyth2010PermutationPValues}.
