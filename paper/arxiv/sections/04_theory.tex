\section{Theoretical guarantees}
\label{sec:theory}

The formal results in this paper concern only fixed-node Stage~A screening in
the fixed-$B$ reference regime. The node is pre-specified, the tested feature
family is label-independent, and p-values are computed with the $+1$ Monte
Carlo correction and the procedure's conservative tie rule. We do not claim a
general inference theory for the fully adaptive tree or forest.

\subsection{Setup}
\label{sec:theory-setup}

Fix a node $t$ with data $(X_t,Y_t)$ and tested feature family $F_t$, where
$m_t := |F_t|$. All probability statements below are read conditional on
$(X_t,U)$, with $U$ denoting label-independent algorithmic randomness as in
\cref{app:assumptions}. The relevant null is the nodewise \emph{complete}
permutation null A0.2: conditional on $(X_t,U)$, the label vector $Y_t$ is
exchangeable under the permutation scheme used by Stage~A.

This target is intentionally narrow. It is a calibration statement for one
pre-specified screening problem, not a partial-null featurewise claim when
other covariates carry signal. It is also not a selective-inference result for
Stage~B thresholds, internal nodes, or early-stopped outputs; those are
treated elsewhere in the manuscript as adaptive statistics.

\subsection{Super-uniformity of fixed-B permutation p-values}
\label{sec:theory-plusone}

\begin{theorem}[$+1$ permutation p-values are super-uniform]
\label{them:plusone-superuniform}
Assume A0.1--A0.5 (\cref{app:assumptions}). In particular, under the null being
tested, the permutation statistics $(T_0,T_1,\dots,T_B)$ are exchangeable
conditional on $(X_t,U)$. Define the right-tail $+1$ Monte Carlo p-value
%
\begin{equation}
  p_{\mathrm{MC}}^{\ge}
  := \frac{1 + \sum_{b=1}^B \ind\{T_b \ge T_0\}}{B+1}.
\end{equation}
%
Then for every $\alpha \in [0,1]$,
%
\begin{equation}
  \PP(p_{\mathrm{MC}}^{\ge} \le \alpha \mid X_t,U) \le \alpha.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
This is the calibration backbone for fixed-$B$ Stage~A screening. It is the
classical exchangeable-rank argument for Monte Carlo permutation tests,
adapted to the ``identity plus $B$ random permutations'' computation used in
the procedure. The left-tail version follows by applying the same statement to
$-T$; see \cref{app:permutation-exchangeability,app:proof-plusone}.

\subsection{Immediate decision-level consequences}
\label{sec:theory-stageA}

\begin{corollary}[Stage~A complete-null control at a fixed node]
\label{cor:stageA-global-null}
Assume A0.1--A0.5 (\cref{app:assumptions}). Under the nodewise complete null at
$t$ (A0.2), let Stage~A reject when
%
\begin{equation}
  \min_{j \in F_t} p_{t,j} < \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}/m_t.
\end{equation}
%
Then
%
\begin{equation}
  \PP\!\left(\exists j \in F_t : p_{t,j} < \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}/m_t \,\middle|\, X_t,U\right)
  \le \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}.
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
This corollary controls the probability of any Stage~A rejection at the fixed
node under the complete permutation null. It is a statement about the nodewise
decision event, not a calibrated p-value for the selected feature
$j_t^\star$, and it does not imply strong partial-null FWER control when some
features are truly associated with $Y_t$.

\subsection{A root-level consequence}
\label{sec:theory-root}

\begin{remark}[Optional structural consequence]
If a tree grown in fixed-$B$ mode contains at least one internal split, then
the root must have rejected in Stage~A. Combining this event inclusion with
\cref{cor:stageA-global-null} at the root yields
%
\begin{equation}
  \PP(\text{the fitted tree has at least one internal split} \mid X_{\mathrm{root}},U)
  \le \alpha_{\mathrm{sel}}.
\end{equation}
\end{remark}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
This is the cleanest whole-tree consequence available from the fixed-node
theory. Under a complete root null, the tree cannot split at all without first
passing the root Stage~A screen. The statement still stops at the root: it does
not calibrate internal-node quantities, Stage~B threshold searches, or forest
behavior under resampling schemes that break exchangeability.
