\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper studies whether the runtime of CIT and CIF can be reduced without
reducing ranking quality. At a fixed node with a fixed permutation budget, the
Stage~A result gives Bonferroni control of the nodewise rejection probability.
That p-value interpretation applies to the local test; the full adaptive tree
and forest are evaluated empirically. In the CIF ablations, the clearest
runtime controls are adaptive stopping and histogram thresholding. The CIT
timing results are mixed and are therefore reported separately from the CIF
runtime and downstream score results.

On the classification benchmark, CIF remains useful for feature ranking after
the runtime reductions studied here and improves on ctree, cforest, and CIT.
The high-$p$ and synthetic results show two patterns: CIF is stronger at
enriching the selected top-$k$ feature set with informative features than at
placing an informative feature first, and the main sparse high-$p$ limit is
feature sampling inside the forest. Further progress likely depends on making
informative features available at split decisions more often while preserving
the nodewise feature selection rule that makes the method distinctive.
