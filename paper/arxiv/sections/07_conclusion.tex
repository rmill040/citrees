\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper evaluates conditional-inference tree and forest rankings for top-$k$
downstream prediction and proves a fixed-node guarantee for Stage~A permutation
testing. The experiments support CIF as a useful feature-ranking method,
especially in classification: its rankings compare favorably with ctree,
cforest, and CIT, and they remain competitive among stronger tree-ensemble
baselines.

The ablations sharpen that empirical picture. Adaptive stopping and bounded
threshold search preserve the practical runtime profile, while the forest rather
than a one-tree variant carries most of the ranking quality. The high-$p$ and
synthetic analyses add the main diagnostic caution: sparse forests should be
checked for whether informative features reach the split-test candidate sets
often enough to enter the top-$k$ ranking. Overall, CIF is best viewed here as a
statistically motivated, empirically benchmarked feature-ranking procedure for
downstream prediction.
