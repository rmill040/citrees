\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper evaluates conditional-inference tree and forest rankings for top-$k$
downstream prediction and proves a fixed-node guarantee for Stage~A permutation
testing. The experiments support CIF as a top-$k$ feature-ranking method,
especially in classification: its rankings compare favorably with ctree,
cforest, and CIT, and they remain competitive among stronger tree-ensemble
baselines.

The ablations add two practical details. Adaptive stopping and bounded threshold
search avoid large runtime increases in CIF, while the one-tree ablation has the
largest ranking-quality loss. The high-$p$ and synthetic analyses add two
diagnostic cautions: sparse forests should be checked for whether informative
features reach the split-test candidate sets often enough to enter the top-$k$
ranking, and CIF should not be treated as a first-feature discovery method when
the goal is to identify a single driver. Overall, these results support CIF as a
feature-ranking method for downstream prediction, backed by a local Stage~A
result and benchmark evidence.
