\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper isolates one principled core of conditional inference and shows how
to make it usable. At a pre-specified node, the exhaustive fixed-$B$ Stage~A
$+1$ Monte Carlo p-value is super-uniform, and Bonferroni controls the
probability of any Stage~A rejection in the tested family. In the implemented
learner, adaptive stopping provides the main speedup with near-zero
quality loss, histogram thresholding provides a second speedup by bounding threshold search,
feature scanning provides a smaller additional speedup inside early stopping, and
the broader timing surface shows threshold scanning, muting, and bootstrap to
have smaller effects on speed.

Empirically, CIF is the strongest conditional-inference method in the benchmark.
It clearly improves on the classical conditional-inference baselines and
remains competitive with common tree ensembles on real tabular tasks. The
boundary diagnostics show where that pattern weakens: CIF is strongest at
intermediate budgets and weaker when forest subsampling makes informative
features too hard to expose. The next step is to improve candidate exposure
inside forests while
retaining the nodewise feature-selection rule that makes the method distinctive.
